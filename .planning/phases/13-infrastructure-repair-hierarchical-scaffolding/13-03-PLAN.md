### Key Structural Facts:

1. **Uniform padding confirmed**: All blocks are padded to `MAX_TRIALS_PER_BLOCK = 100` via `pad_block_to_max()`. Stacked arrays have shape `(n_blocks, 100)`. Every `*_block_likelihood` call receives arrays of shape `(100,)` regardless of actual trial count.

2. **lax.scan output shape**: Since each block is padded to 100 trials, `log_probs` from `lax.scan` always has shape `(100,)`. Masked/padding trials have `log_prob_masked = 0.0` (because `valid = mask[t]` and `log_prob_masked = log_prob * valid`).

3. **fori_loop body_fn pattern**: All 6 stacked wrappers use `body_fn(block_idx, total_ll)` with dynamic indexing `stimuli_stacked[block_idx]`. The body returns `total_ll + block_ll` (scalar accumulation only).

4. **JIT wrappers**: Only 2 exist — `q_learning_block_likelihood_jit` (L621, `static_argnums=(6, 7, 8)`) and `wmrl_block_likelihood_jit` (L1607, `static_argnums=(10, 11, 12, 13)`). Both use positional `static_argnums`, NOT `static_argnames`. No JIT wrappers exist for stacked functions.

5. **lax.scan produces uniform-shape outputs**: Because all blocks are padded to 100 trials, `lax.scan` over block indices will produce `all_block_probs` of shape `(num_blocks, 100)` — uniform and valid for scan accumulation.
