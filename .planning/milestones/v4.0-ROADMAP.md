# Milestone v4.0: Hierarchical Bayesian Pipeline & LBA Acceleration

**Status:** ✅ SHIPPED 2026-04-19
**Phases:** 13-22 (10 phases)
**Total Plans:** 47

## Overview

Move the inference pipeline from MLE point estimates to full hierarchical Bayesian posteriors with trauma subscales as Level-2 predictors, principled Bayesian model comparison (PSIS-LOO + stacking + RFX-BMS/PXP), and GPU-accelerated LBA sampling for M4. Replace post-hoc FDR correction (48-test M6b family) with a single joint posterior and replace r<0.80 MLE recovery with shrinkage-regularized individual-level posteriors.

Anchored to Baribault & Collins (2023, *Psychological Methods*) and Hess et al. (2025, *Computational Psychiatry*) for the Bayesian model-selection workflow (Phase 21), Yao, Vehtari, Simpson, Gelman (2018) for stacking weights, and Rigoux et al. (2014) / Stephan et al. (2009) for the secondary RFX-BMS track.

Phases 19-20 added in-flight to research GPU-accelerated likelihood via associative scan and DEER non-linear parallelization. Phase 21 added 2026-04-12 to replace the MLE-preselected winner workflow with a fully Bayesian step-by-step pipeline. Phase 22 added 2026-04-19 as closure (verification, traceability, reproducibility).

## Milestone Summary

**Key Decisions:**

- **Single-stage hierarchical Bayesian (NOT two-stage PEB):** All models fit as single-stage via NumPyro NUTS. Individual parameters, group priors, and Level-2 regression coefficients sampled simultaneously in one MCMC run. Rationale: uncertainty propagates correctly, shrinkage via partial pooling, L2 coefficients tempered by L1 uncertainty.
- **NumPyro-only backend:** PyMC dropped entirely in Phase 13 (INFRA-07). Reduces stack complexity; consistent JIT semantics.
- **Fully-batched vmap pattern:** Post-Phase 20, all 6 choice-only hierarchical models emit a single `numpyro.factor("obs", per_participant_ll.sum())` via nested `jax.vmap` (outer=participants, inner=blocks). Zero per-participant factor sites remaining.
- **CPU chain_method for choice-only; GPU for M4 only:** True `jax.pmap` parallel is only worth the GPU for the M4 LBA fit. For choice-only 4-chain MCMC, CPU parallel is correct (vmap on GPU was 7-13× slower per cluster benchmark).
- **Primary Bayesian model comparison: PSIS-LOO + stacking weights (Yao 2018).** AIC/BIC deprecated for hierarchical-Bayesian selection. Secondary: RFX-BMS with PXP (Stephan 2009; Rigoux 2014).
- **Collins K bounds [2,6] with phi_approx transform:** K parameterization locked via `docs/K_PARAMETERIZATION.md`; `parameterization_version="v4.0-K[2,6]-phiapprox"` stamped on every output CSV. Load-side validation via `config.load_fits_with_validation`.
- **Cohort = N=138** (intersection of task completeness ≥ 400 trials, late-block accuracy ≥ 0.50, non-NA on 5 required survey columns). Down from N=154 task-only.
- **Principled priors:** All `mu_prior_loc` = 0.0 (Φ(0) = 0.5 on bounded scale; 95% prior interval ~[0.02, 0.98] given `sigma_pr ~ HalfNormal(0.2)`). Previous MLE-calibrated priors retained in `_PRIOR_LEGACY_MLE_CALIBRATED` for sensitivity runs.
- **Subscale L2 design: 4 predictors, condition number 11.3** (IES-R hyperarousal residual dropped — exact linear dependence). Gram-Schmidt residualized intrusion/avoidance subscales vs. IES-R total.
- **Stick-breaking for M6b dual perseveration** (rather than inequality-constrained joint sampling): enforces κ + κ_s ≤ 1 by construction; avoids boundary-reflection pathology.
- **M4 kept in scope** despite research recommendation to descope: accept ~150-200 GPU-hour budget, ~24-48h single-fit wall clock, and near-certain Pareto-k fallback. Phase 17 sequenced after Phase 16 so M6b's non-centered pattern serves as M4's template.
- **DEER no-go decision:** Documented with empirical evidence in Phase 20. Phase 19's hybrid (O(log T) scan + sequential post-scan) is the final GPU path.
- **Cluster-execution framing:** Every cluster-pending deliverable references `bash cluster/21_submit_pipeline.sh` or `bash cluster/12_submit_all_gpu.sh` as the cold-start entry. No piecemeal sbatch instructions anywhere in closure docs.
